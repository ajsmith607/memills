

I am doing historical research that includes information on Christ's First Presbyterian Church in Hempstead, and in particular, an intriguing mystery surrounding your pastor from 1850 to 1860, Reverend Dr. Nathaniel Clark Locke (1815 to 21 Jul 1862). I write to ask for your assistance as I attempt to solve this mystery.

The Reverend's image can be found between pages 6 and 7 of the volume "Souvenir of the 250th Anniversary of Christ's First Presbyterian Church" available on the Church's website, and I have attached a cropped version of that page to this message. Knowing what the Reverend looked like from this, I was recently shocked to find a photograph of the Reverend that appears to show him exactly as he appears in the Souvenir book, down to the very same cloths, but posed slightly differently. However, this other photograph identifies the man as someone else, one of the Reverend's congregants in fact. 

Clearly, the same person is in each image, but I believe the identification of Reverend Locke in the Sourvenir book is correct, and I am asking for your assistance in my attempt to prove this theory and publish it to benefit those in the future. 

If possible, I would like to locate the original artifact used for the Souvenir book image, and either have it scanned or photographed, front and back. I would like to do the same for any other images of the Reverend that you might have yourself or might know the existence of in another historical archive. I hope to be able to date the original artifact as well as corroborate the Reverend's identification. 

I realize that the artifact may not have survived more than a century later, and that just looking for it, let alone scanning it, may be a significant effort. If it is appropriate to do so, I will make a donation to the church in acknowledgement of this effort. 

I would finally like permission to publish (with appropriate citation of course) any such scans or photographs of the artifacts on my website at memills.com, where my published research is publicly available for free. On the site, you will see a page about Hempstead that explains the signficance of Reverend Locke within my scope of interest. I am currently working on a more complete biography of the Reverend.

Thank you for considering my request.







The Reverend’s biography in New York seems pretty simple, he came from Virginia to Central Presbyterian Church in Brooklyn at the end of 1847, then by 1850 he is said to be in Hempstead, there until 1860, then he dies in 1862:

“Installation.–Rev. N. C. Locke, late of Northhampton, Va. was installed Pastor of the Central Presbyterian church, in Willoughby corner of Peral street, this city, on Wednesday evening, 22d inst.” ( 12 Dec 1847, The Brooklyn Daily Eagle and Kings County Democrat, p2, nyshistoricnewspapers.org.)

Thinking through the relevant actors and facts:

  - Juliett (Loper) Shepard (06 Jan 1845 to 02 Apr 1911) 
      - Juliett was 11 years old when grandfather Luther died.

  - Luther Loper (04 Jun 1780 to 05 Apr 1856)
      - Luther is a member of Rev. Locke's church in Hempstead.
      - Luther is 35 years older than Rev. Locke.

  - Reverend Nathaniel Clark Locke (1815 to 21 Jul 1862)
      - Luther’s pastor from 1850 until Luther's death in 1856.
      - Died only 6 years after Luther.
      - Souvenir Book published in 1895, 33 years after Rev. N. C. Locke died, along with another source, gives his service in Hempstead from 1850 to 1860, and he died in 1862.
